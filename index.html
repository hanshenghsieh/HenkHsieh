Between Two Lines | Informational Platform

Between Two Lines is a final year project that tried to tackle and destigmatise the unintended pregnancy issue among teenagers and young adults in Taiwan by developing an informational platform that provides legitimate knowledge for people in need to understand the options and related consequences.

Regarding the current sex education in Taiwan, despite the schools have managed to pull off the fundamental knowledge of contraception, we noticed that what happens after finding out the pregnancy in young age is still pretty much absent in our textbooks and educational system. Therefore, we decided to build a new online platform with the aim to design a better experience that can truly help people in need understand everything they need to know about unintended pregnancy.

In this project, I was in charge of User Experience Design (UX), User Interface Design (UI), Infographic Visualisation, Illustration and Usability Testing.

2020 New Year Postcard

Every year Taiwan Startup Stadium sends out Lunar New Year gifts along with the best wishes to their fellow startup members and business partners before the New Year. This year was no exception. With the inspiration from the Asian tradition, the initial pattern of the design symbolises the shape of Rat, which is the horoscope sign of the year. The colour scheme inherits the traditional cultural codes that represent fortune and prosperous with lower saturation and brightness. I tried to make the design simple, different from the typical New Year celebration vibe and more inclusive that not only can be used on Lunar New Year occasions but also the year of 2020 itself.

2018 National Chiao Tung University Undokai

Originally from Japan, the spirit of Undokai, the sports day, isn't merely about individual honour and competition but also team-bonding and cooperation. In order to encourage as many people as possible, Undokai also offers a series of light-hearted team-building activities for everyone to be part of the event.

Initially, the design of the poster utilised balls and running track as symbols to depict the imagery of track and field events respectively. After a few editions, I decided to combine the two elements altogether to represent one of the most common team-building games in Undokai - Odamakorogashi, Giant Balls Rolling, which requires a team to work together and roll the giant rubber ball towards the finish line - to portray this underline spirit of Undokai.

However, mathematics isn't merely about calculating or logic, it is also capable of making plenty of splendid visual artworks with the use of geometrical methods. Professor Chen Ming Zhang, therefore, decided to hold this exhibition and introduced the concept of the ‚ÄúStructural Cloning‚Äù to the public by showing people the amazing artworks that have been done accordingly and explained the theory behind it.
